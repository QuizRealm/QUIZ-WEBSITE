The Holiday Archives

Origins

Why December 25th? It was originally the Roman festival of 'Sol Invictus' (The Unconquered Sun). The date was chosen to coincide with winter solstice celebrations, merging pagan traditions with the new Christian holiday.

Cinema

The "Die Hard" Debate: While released in July 1988, director John McTiernan confirmed it is a Christmas movie. It features 4 distinct Christmas songs and the plot relies entirely on the office holiday party.

Psychology

Nostalgia triggers: The smell of pine and cinnamon triggers the olfactory bulb, which is directly connected to the brain's amygdala (emotion). This is why holiday memories feel more "vivid" than others.

Music

The Gold Standard: Bing Crosby's "White Christmas" remains the best-selling single of all time, with over 50 million copies sold worldwide. It was written by Irving Berlin in 1940.

Folklore

Odin's Hunt: Before Santa had reindeer, the Norse god Odin rode an eight-legged horse named Sleipnir across the midwinter sky. Children would leave carrots in boots for the horse—the origin of stockings.

Quiz Strategy

Looking to ace the Impossible Quotes quiz? Remember that many modern classics (like *Elf*) reference older movies (*It's a Wonderful Life*) in their dialogue.
