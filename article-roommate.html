Living with someone is the ultimate stress test for a relationship. It doesn't matter if they are your best friend, your partner, or a stranger from the internet—sharing a bathroom changes everything.

The mistake most people make is focusing on "friendship compatibility" instead of "lifestyle compatibility." You can love grabbing drinks with someone but hate how they load the dishwasher. This guide acts as a filtering algorithm. We break down the unspoken rules of co-habitation into data points you can measure.

01 //

The "Big 5" Friction Points

Cleanliness Threshold

The #1 cause of arguments. Define "clean." Does it mean "sanitize daily" or "no visible mold"?

Financial Reliability

Rent is just the start. Discuss utilities, grocery splitting, and what happens if one person loses a job.

Noise & Schedules

Are you a night owl living with a 5 AM runner? Mismatched sleep cycles create deep resentment.

Guest Policy

The "partners staying over" rule. 3 nights a week? Every night? This needs a hard number.

Phase 02 // Data Collection

The Interrogation Protocol

Polite conversation hides red flags. These 29 specific data points are designed to expose lifestyle incompatibilities before you sign the lease.

  • How long can dishes stay in the sink? (Hours vs. Days)
  • What is your ideal thermostat setting? (Winter/Summer)
  • "Clean as you go" or "Big clean on Sundays"?
  • How often do you take out the trash?
  • Which household chore do you absolutely refuse to do?
  • Shoes inside the house: Yes or No?
  • How do you handle expiring food in the fridge?
  • Bathroom cleaning schedule: Weekly or "When it looks dirty"?
  • Do you work from home in common areas?
  • Personal items in the living room: Clutter tolerance level?
  • Absolute max budget for Rent + Utilities?
  • Shared consumables (TP, Oil, Spices): Split or Buy Your Own?
  • Current income stability/Job security status?
  • Have you ever been late on rent? (Be honest).
  • Whose name goes on the utility bills?
  • Furniture policy: Who owns the couch if we move out?
  • Subletting policy for long travel periods?
  • Partner Sleepover Limit: How many nights/week?
  • Hosting: Do you like parties? How often?
  • Dynamic: Looking for a "Best Friend" or "Co-existent Ghost"?
  • Guest Notice: Text me 1 hour before or 1 day before?
  • Substance use at home (Drinking/Smoking)?
  • Pets: Allergies or hidden desire to get a puppy?
  • Quiet Hours: When does the house shut down?
  • Conflict Style: Passive aggressive notes or face-to-face?
  • Biggest Pet Peeve when living with others?
  • Stress Reaction: Do you isolate or vent?
  • House Meetings: Monthly grievance airing (Yay/Nay)?
  • Feedback: How do you want to be told you're being annoying?

The Red Flags

Sometimes people lie. Or they lack self-awareness. Watch for these subtle behavioral cues during the interview process. If you spot more than two, run.

Flag #1

"I'm super chill/laid back about everything."

Translation: I will never clean anything, and I will call you "uptight" when you ask me to do dishes.

Flag #2

They badmouth all previous roommates.

Translation: They were the problem in every scenario. You will be the next villain in their story.

Flag #3

"I'm currently between jobs/freelancing is slow."

Translation: They might be great people, but can they pay rent next month? High risk.

Flag #4

They interrupt you constantly.

Translation: They lack respect for boundaries. This will translate to eating your food and entering your room.

The Agreement

Handshakes don't hold up in court (or at 2 AM arguments). We recommend drafting a simple "Roommate Constitution." It doesn't need to be legally binding, but it needs to be written.

  • Quiet Hours: Defined times (e.g., 11 PM - 7 AM).
  • Guest Limit: Max nights per week for partners.
  • Thermostat Settings: Agreed upon range.
  • Cleaning Rotation: Who does what, and when.
  • Food Policy: "Share everything" vs "Touch my milk and die."
