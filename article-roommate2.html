PHASE: VETTING

The Psychological Interrogation.

Stop asking "Are you clean?" Everyone says yes. The human brain is wired to project an idealized version of self during social interviews. To get the truth, you must pivot from "aspirational" questions to "behavioral" ones. Instead of asking about their cleanliness, ask: "Walk me through your Sunday routine." If they mention laundry, meal prep, and scrubbing the floors, they are compatible. If they mention sleeping until 2 PM and ordering UberEats, you have your answer.

The "Scenario Test" is your best weapon. Ask them: "The sink is full of dishes, and I'm gone for the weekend. What happens?" Their immediate gut reaction—whether it's annoyance, indifference, or a solution—will tell you more about their conflict resolution style than any standard interview question. We designed the Red Flag Radar to automate this process, analyzing 20 specific micro-behaviors that predict future toxicity. Don't rely on "vibes." Rely on data.

Key search metric: 74% of roommate conflicts stem from undefined expectations regarding "common areas." Establish the "No-Go Zones" immediately. If you work from home in the living room, say it now. If they expect a 24/7 open-door policy, find out now.

PHASE: AUDIT

Digital Forensics Check.

Social Media is a background check. Before you sign a 12-month legal contract, you have a fiduciary duty to yourself to perform a digital audit. This isn't stalking; it's due diligence. Scan their Instagram Stories highlights—not for aesthetics, but for lifestyle compatibility. Do they post "drama" vague-booking stories at 3 AM? That behavior comes home with them. Do they have a history of job hopping visible on LinkedIn? Financial instability affects your rent payment.

The "Ex-Factor" Analysis: How do they talk about their previous roommates? If every past roommate was "crazy," "psycho," or "the worst," you are looking at the common denominator. This is a classic narcissist trait. Our Identity Lab breaks down these archetypes (The Victim, The Main Character, The Gaslighter) to help you categorize potential candidates before they move in.

The Credit Score Taboo: Break it. Ask for proof of income. A "cool" roommate with a 450 credit score is an eviction notice waiting to happen. Normalizing financial transparency is the first step to a successful co-living arrangement. If they hesitate to show proof of stability, that is the loudest Red Flag you will ever hear.

PHASE: CONTRACT

The Roommate Prenup.

Handshakes don't hold up in court. Or at 2 AM when the music is blasting. You need a "Roommate Constitution." This document sits outside the lease but rules the house. It must cover the "Trifecta of Doom": Guests, Thermostats, and Dishes. Define "Overnight Guest"—is it 3 nights a week? Does a boyfriend effectively live there if he showers there every morning? Define the financial penalty for late utilities.

The Exit Strategy: The most important clause is the breakup clause. What happens if one of you wants to leave early? Who is responsible for finding a sub-letter? Establishing these rules while you still like each other prevents legal warfare when things go south. Use our Compatibility Test results as the foundation for this document. If you scored low on "Noise Tolerance," write strict Quiet Hours into the pact.

The "Nuclear Option": Discuss eviction protocols. If someone steals food, stops paying rent, or brings illegal substances into the home, what is the agreed-upon timeline for removal? It sounds harsh, but clarity is kindness. A 30-day notice period written on paper saves friendships and deposits.
