Origins

Action Comics #1: The Golden Age began in 1938 with the debut of Superman. A near-mint copy of this comic sold for $3.2 million.

Cinema History

The Blade Effect: While X-Men (2000) is credited with starting the modern era, 1998's *Blade* was the first successful Marvel theatrical release, proving R-rated heroes could work.

The MCU

Improvised Genius: Robert Downey Jr.'s line "I am Iron Man" at the end of the first film was completely ad-libbed, changing the course of the entire franchise.

Villains

Heath Ledger's Joker: He designed the makeup himself, reasoning that the Joker would apply it messily. The film crew had to replicate his design exactly every day.

Materials

Vibranium vs Adamantium: Vibranium absorbs vibration (Captain America's shield), while Adamantium is virtually indestructible (Wolverine's skeleton).
