Briefing #005 Global Logistics

The Bloodline:
Arteries of Trade.

If the world is a body, these are the veins. 90% of everything you own traveled on these invisible highways.

The ocean is not a blank space. It is a tightly regulated industrial zone. At any given moment, there are over 50,000 merchant ships traversing the high seas, carrying everything from crude oil to iPhones.

The map above visualizes one year of GPS transponder data from these vessels. It reveals that global trade does not wander; it follows precise, narrow corridors dictated by currents, fuel efficiency, and political stability. These are the Sea Lines of Communication (SLOCs).

The Choke Points

The vulnerability of the modern world is visible on this map. Notice how the chaotic web of the open ocean funnels into tiny, dangerous bottlenecks: the Suez Canal, the Panama Canal, and the Strait of Hormuz.

A single ship stuck in the Suez Canal (like the *Ever Given* in 2021) can freeze 12% of global trade instantly. This map is not just about economics; it is the primary blueprint for naval warfare strategy.

About The Dataset

This visualization aggregates 12 months of AIS (Automatic Identification System) data. Every commercial vessel over 300 tons is required to broadcast its position every few seconds.

This map reveals the "Marine Silk Road." It highlights not only the density of traffic but the incredible precision of modern navigation. Ships do not just float across the ocean; they travel in lanes as defined as any highway on land.

QuizRealm Intelligence uses this data to demonstrate the concept of supply chain fragility and global interconnectedness.
